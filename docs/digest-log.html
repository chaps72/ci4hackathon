Bottom line: The administration is moving to fundamentally restructure federal science funding in ways that could cut money to research universities, while research-security restrictions on Chinese partners are tightening—both warrant immediate attention. - **Science funding overhaul** – The White House proposed the most sweeping restructuring of federal research in 80+ years, centering AI and seeking alternatives to peer review, with major agencies ($3B+ FY2026 R&D) required to submit implementation plans within 90 days. - **OSTP "Golden Age" report** – Kratsios's report recommends significantly reducing university research funding and redirecting it to individual scientists and new mission-driven organizations, directly threatening our institutional funding model. - **Pentagon blacklist expansion** – Fudan and Shanghai Jiao Tong were added to the DoD threat list, so any collaborations or personnel ties with these universities now carry heightened compliance and security risk. 2026-07-22
Bottom line: AHRQ is abruptly cancelling already-appropriated health services research grants, putting active awards and multi-year funding at immediate risk for any team with AHRQ support. - **AHRQ grant terminations** - AHRQ sent near-identical "non-award"/cancellation letters ending congressionally appropriated funding, with STAT/AcademyHealth citing at least 78 grants (~$200M total, $109.2M remaining) affected as the agency realigns to White House priorities, so any PI holding an AHRQ award should assume possible disruption and flag exposure now. Both source reports (Becker's, 7/20; STAT, 7/22) describe the same terminations and are consistent on the core facts.
