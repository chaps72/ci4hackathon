White House science funding overhaul
On July 22, 2026, the White House unveiled a science funding overhaul, followed the next day by a science report calling for reduced university research funding. The initiative currently signals a policy direction toward cutting federal support for university-based research. This could mean significant funding pressures ahead for university research operations.

Pentagon blacklist of foreign universities
On July 23, 2026, the Pentagon expanded its blacklist of foreign universities to include China's top civilian institutions. The action currently broadens restrictions to encompass leading civilian, not just military-linked, universities. This may complicate or restrict research collaborations and partnerships with the affected Chinese institutions.
2026-07-23Pentagon expands blacklist to include China's top civilian universities (News)
AHRQ grant terminations
On July 20, 2026, reports emerged that AHRQ had terminated dozens of patient care research grants, followed by confirmation on July 22 that the federal healthcare quality agency canceled $109 million in grants. The situation reflects a significant and recent funding withdrawal affecting healthcare quality research. For our research office, this signals potential exposure for any active or pending AHRQ-funded projects and warrants immediate review of affected awards.
